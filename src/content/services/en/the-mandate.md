## The problem it solves

Most strategic failures are not execution failures. The execution was often excellent. The failure was in the thinking that preceded it — in the assumptions that everyone had agreed on so thoroughly that they had stopped being examined.

By the time those assumptions fail, the cost is already sunk. The question you're left with is: why didn't we ask this earlier?

The Mandate is designed to answer that question before the cost is incurred.

### What eight weeks looks like

The engagement runs in three phases.

**Weeks 1–3: Surface**
We work through your current strategic position systematically — identifying every belief your strategy depends on being true, from the obvious to the invisible. This includes individual sessions with key leaders, team sessions where the full picture can be assembled, and a documented map of everything we've found.

**Weeks 4–6: Test**
Each assumption is stress-tested. We apply the strongest available counter-evidence, model the scenarios where each assumption fails, and rank them by the damage they would cause. Some will prove solid. Some will not.

**Weeks 7–8: Capability**
We don't hand you a report and leave. We work through the implications for your strategy, document the revised thinking, and run the team through the process itself — so you can apply it independently to the next major decision.

### What you're not buying

You are not buying a consulting report. You are not buying a strategic plan. You are not buying validation of the direction already chosen.

You are buying the answer to the question that should precede all of those things: *what are we treating as certain that deserves scrutiny?*

### Scope and duration

The Mandate runs for eight weeks. Long enough to do the work at depth. Short enough that the deliverable is clear and the engagement has a defined end. No dependency, no open-ended commitment.
