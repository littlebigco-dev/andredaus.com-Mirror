## The problem it solves

Most AI failures are not technology failures. The technology often delivered exactly what was specified. The failure was in the specification — in the assumptions about what problem was worth solving, what outcome would look like, and whether the people and processes were ready.

By the time those assumptions surface, the investment has already been made. The question everyone is left with: why didn't we ask this earlier?

The AI Due Diligence Mandate is designed to answer that question before the cost is incurred.

### What eight weeks looks like

The engagement runs in three phases.

**Weeks 1–3: Surface**

We work through your AI strategy systematically — identifying every belief it depends on being true, from the obvious to the invisible. This includes individual sessions with key stakeholders, team sessions where the full picture can be assembled, and a documented map of everything we've found. Vendor claims. Internal assumptions. Strategic rationale. Competitive logic. All of it examined.

**Weeks 4–6: Test**

Each assumption is stress-tested. We apply the strongest available counter-evidence, model the scenarios where each assumption fails, and rank them by the damage they would cause. Some will prove solid. Some will not. The ones that don't get documented before more budget is committed to them.

**Weeks 7–8: Capability**

We don't hand you a report and leave. We work through the implications for your AI strategy, document the revised thinking, and run your team through the process itself — so you can apply it independently to every significant AI decision that follows.

### What you're not buying

You are not buying an AI strategy. You are not buying a vendor recommendation. You are not buying validation of a direction already chosen.

You are buying the answer to the question that should precede all of those things: *what are we treating as certain that deserves scrutiny before we commit more to it?*

### Scope and duration

The AI Due Diligence Mandate runs for eight weeks. Long enough to do the work at depth across a complex AI investment landscape. Short enough that the deliverable is clear and the engagement has a defined end.

No dependency. No open-ended commitment. No follow-on work unless you want it.
