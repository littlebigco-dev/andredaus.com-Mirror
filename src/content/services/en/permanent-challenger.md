## The problem it solves

The more senior you become, the less honest feedback you receive. Not because the people around you are dishonest — because the incentives don't support honesty. Your team is working toward the same goals. Your advisors want the relationship. Your investors want the return.

Nobody in that room is paid to tell you that your reasoning is wrong.

This engagement installs that function.

### What the sessions look like

Two sessions per month, sixty minutes each. There is no fixed agenda. You bring whatever is live: a decision you're navigating, a pattern you keep noticing, a situation that isn't resolving the way it should.

I ask the questions that don't get asked in your regular meetings. We find the assumption underneath the presenting problem. We test whether your current thinking is solid or whether it's carrying a load-bearing belief that hasn't been examined.

Sessions are not recorded.

### What changes over time

Leaders who stay with this engagement for 3–6 months typically describe the same shift: they stop being surprised by things that, in retrospect, were obvious. Not because they had better information — because they had a different relationship with their own certainty.

That's the outcome this is designed to produce.

### What this is not

Not coaching. Not mentoring. Not a sounding board in the conventional sense — because a sounding board listens and reflects, and that's not what this is.

This is structured adversarial advisory. The sessions are not comfortable. That's what makes them useful.
