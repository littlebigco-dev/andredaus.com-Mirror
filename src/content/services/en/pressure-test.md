## The problem it solves

AI vendor proposals are written by people who have spent months preparing them. You have days — sometimes hours — to evaluate them.

In that gap, the assumptions go unchallenged. The optimistic projections don't get compared to the failure cases that didn't make the case study. The dependencies the proposal doesn't mention — the data quality, the change management, the integration complexity — stay invisible.

Nobody in your organisation is in a position to read this critically. They're under pressure to move fast, they don't want to be seen as blocking progress, and they may not have the specific experience to identify what's wrong with the logic.

### What you send

Share the vendor proposal, internal roadmap, or investment memo. A brief note on what you're trying to achieve and what's already been decided helps but isn't required.

That's it. No intake call. No questionnaire. No setup.

### What you receive

Within 48–72 hours, a written assessment covering: the assumptions the proposal depends on, the claims that deserve scrutiny, the risks that aren't named, and the questions you should be asking before you commit.

Not a summary. A critical read.

### Why this format

The fastest way to improve a decision is a fast, independent, critical read of the reasoning. This format is designed to be fast enough to actually influence the decision — not thorough enough to become another project.
