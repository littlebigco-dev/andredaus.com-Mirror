## The problem it solves

In 2025, the AI assumption isn't "should we use AI?" — that's already been made. The assumption is *which AI*, *how fast*, and *how much*.

That's the wrong level to question.

Most AI initiatives fail not because of the technology but because of the reasoning that preceded it. "AI will make us more efficient" — efficient at what, exactly? "We'll lose competitive advantage if we don't act" — compared to whom, on which dimension? "Our people are resistant to AI" — or are they noticing something the strategy missed?

These aren't rhetorical questions. They're the questions that should have been asked before the initiative started — and can still be asked before the next phase of investment.

### What this session is not

It is not an AI strategy consultation. I won't tell you which tools to adopt, which vendor to choose, or how to run your rollout. There are plenty of people who will do that — most of them with a stake in your answer.

This session does one specific thing: it finds the assumptions your AI strategy depends on and asks whether any of them deserve scrutiny before you spend more on them.
