## The problem it solves

In 2026, the question isn't whether to use AI. That decision has already been made in most boardrooms. The questions being made now are which AI, how fast, and how much.

That's the wrong level to pressure-test.

Most AI initiatives fail not because of the technology but because of the reasoning that preceded it. "AI will make us more efficient" — efficient at what, exactly? "We'll lose competitive advantage if we don't act" — compared to whom, on which dimension? "Our people are resistant to AI" — or are they noticing something the strategy missed?

These aren't rhetorical questions. They're the questions that should have been asked before the initiative started — and can still be asked before the next phase of investment.

### What this assessment is not

It is not an AI strategy consultation. I won't tell you which tools to adopt, which vendor to choose, or how to manage your rollout. There are plenty of people who will do that — most of them with a stake in your answer.

This assessment does one specific thing: it finds the assumptions your AI strategy depends on and asks whether any of them deserve scrutiny before you spend more on them.

### How it works

You share the relevant documents — the vendor proposal, the internal roadmap, the strategic rationale, whatever exists. We spend one structured call working through the specific AI decision: what you're planning, what you've committed, what assumptions are driving it.

Over 5–7 working days, I work through the initiative systematically. Every vendor claim examined. Every internal assumption named. The strongest available counter-evidence applied to each.

You receive a written assessment. Clear verdict. Documented reasoning.
