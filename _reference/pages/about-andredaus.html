I've always been the one
who asks why.

Not as a rhetorical move. As a reflex. From the time I was old enough to get into trouble for it, I found it genuinely difficult to accept "that's how we do things" as an answer to anything. That discomfort has been professionally useful. It turns out that most organisations — and most of the people running them — are in exactly the same position: there are questions they're not asking, assumptions they haven't examined, and conclusions they've committed to before the evidence was in.

The difference is that I've built a methodology around making that visible. Strategic Opposition isn't contrarianism for its own sake. It's a structured process for identifying the assumptions that are running unchallenged — and then challenging them, systematically, before the market does it for you.

My formative experience was noticing, repeatedly, that the smartest people in a room were also the most confident in conclusions that hadn't been seriously tested. Intelligence doesn't protect you from confirmation bias. It just makes your rationalisations more convincing. The only thing that actually works is structural challenge — someone with a mandate to find what's wrong, who is explicitly not part of the belief system being examined.

That's the role I play. Not consultant. Not coach in the conventional sense. Professional opposition. I'm here to make it impossible to keep avoiding the real question.

"Everyone said it wouldn't work. Then someone came along who didn't know that — and just did it."

A principle, not a quote

This isn't about ignoring expertise. It's about recognising that accumulated consensus often contains assumptions no one has checked recently. The person who doesn't inherit those assumptions has a structural advantage — and it can be engineered deliberately.

How I work

Two brands.
One approach.

I deliberately separated my work into two distinct brands. Not for organisational tidiness — because conflating them would undermine both.

andredaus.com is the Thinking Brand. Strategic Opposition coaching and consulting — for leaders and organisations who need someone outside the belief system, with a mandate to find what's wrong before it finds them.

LittleBig.Co is the Building Brand. Technical consulting, WordPress infrastructure, and IT implementation — for organisations that need things built properly, not just advised on. The separation matters: a client who comes to me for technical implementation shouldn't default to seeing me as just another IT consultant when the real problem is a strategic assumption that needs challenging.
