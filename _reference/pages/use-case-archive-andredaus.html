Why use cases exist

Don't hire me to solve your problem.
Hire me to question whether you've named it correctly.

Most organisations arrive with a well-articulated problem. That problem is usually the symptom. The use cases here are designed to help you recognize your situation — and to show you what's actually underneath it.

Each case is built on real research into common strategic failure modes, examined through the Strategic Opposition lens. The question is never "how do we fix this?" It's always: "what are we assuming that makes this look unfixable?"

  1. P

    Problem — as you'd describe it

    The situation in the language most organisations use. Recognisable enough to create identification. Already slightly wrong.

  2. A

    Agitate — what it actually costs

    Real data. Real failures. Why conventional responses compound the problem rather than solving it. This section is designed to be uncomfortable.

  3. S

    Solution — a different frame

    Not a prescriptive fix. A reframe of what's actually being solved for, and what a Strategic Opposition engagement looks like applied to this specific problem type.
