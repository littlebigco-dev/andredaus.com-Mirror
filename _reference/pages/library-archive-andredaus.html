  • Confirmation Bias

    The tendency to search for, interpret, and remember information that confirms what we already believe — while filtering out, dismissing, or forgetting anything that contradicts it. Operates below the threshold of awareness, which is what makes it structurally dangerous rather than merely inconvenient.

    About this library

    Reference material for thinking that doesn't flatter itself.

    Strategic Opposition isn't a proprietary methodology built on invented terminology. It draws on decades of cognitive science, facilitation research, and decision-making literature — accumulated evidence for why intelligent people and well-run organisations get things consistently wrong.

    This library is the documented foundation. Each entry explains a concept, cross-references related ones, and links to use cases and engagements where the concept appears in practice. The aim is not comprehensiveness for its own sake — it's to make visible the mechanics that Strategic Opposition works to interrupt.

    CB

    Cognitive Biases

    Systematic errors in thinking — patterns where the mind's shortcuts produce reliably wrong outputs. Not quirks of irrational people. Default features of intelligent minds under real conditions.

    LS

    Liberating Structures

    Facilitation methods designed to distribute participation and surface ideas that conventional meeting formats suppress. The structural counterpart to individual bias mitigation.

    M

    Methods

    Thinking frameworks and analytical approaches — pre-mortem, red teaming, inversion, and others — that create structured challenge where unstructured thinking would produce confirmation.
